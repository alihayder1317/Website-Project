About Real Madrid

History

Real Madrid's origins go back to when football was introduced to Madrid by the academics and students of the Institución Libre de Enseñanza, which included several Cambridge and Oxford University graduates. They founded (Sociedad) Sky Football in 1897, commonly known as La Sociedad (The Society) as it was the only one based in Madrid, playing on Sunday mornings at Moncloa. In 1900, conflict between members caused some of them to leave and create a new club, Nueva Sociedad de Football (New Society of Football), to distinguish themselves from Sky Football. Among the dissenters were Julián Palacios, recognized as the first Real Madrid president, Juan Padrós and Carlos Padrós, the latter two being brothers and future presidents of Real Madrid. In 1901, this new club was renamed as Madrid Football Club.

Rivalries

One of Real Madrid's biggest rivals is FC Barcelona. Over the last few decades they contiue to face off in a match known as the El Clasico in which fans across Madrid and Barcelona come together at the Santiago Bernabeu to watch this incredible rivalry. The matches never fail to disappoint as they bring a great amount of suspense, action and spectators. These matches also represent the cultural and political tensions that are felt between people from both cities.

Famous Player

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro, born 5 February 1985, is a Portuguese professional footballer who plays as a forward for and captained Read Madrid for 9 years and the Portugal national team. He is considered by many as one of the greatest footballers of our generation puttting him to the likes of players like Pele. After captaining Real Madrid for 9 years he became known as a legend of the club breaking numerous records across the board.
